THE FIRST SPECIES OF CATS ON EARTH

Biologists have traced most living animals in the order carnivora to a small group of mammals called miacids, which lived 33 to 65 million years ago. Miacids lived in the forests of what is now called North America and Eurasia.
The Proailuris is the oldest known felid (aka: another name for feline or cat). It did not appear in the fossil record until 30 million years ago and lived in what is now called Europe. They had already evolved some of the hallmarks of their feline descendants.
The pseudaelurus is a successor to proailrus. It first appeared around 20 million years ago. Psedaelurus was also the first felid to venture into North America. Its features included a slender body, short snout, rudder-like tail, and agile claws suitable for climbing. These species included the lynx-sized Pseudaelurus Transitorius and the cougar-sized Pseudaelurus Quadrientatus.
